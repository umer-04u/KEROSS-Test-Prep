Behavioral Questions: The STAR Method

Use this structure for "Tell me about a time..." questions.

S
Situation
"During my final year project..."
T
Task
"We had to fix a database error..."
A
Action
"I debugged the code using logs..."
R
Result
"The app ran successfully."

High-Risk HR Traps üö©

"Why should we NOT hire you?"
Safe Answer: "If you need an expert immediately, I may not fit. But for a fresher eager to learn, I am the right choice."
"What if you get a higher salary elsewhere?"
Safe Answer: "For my first job, learning and training are more important than salary. I prefer your company's growth path."
"Do you have any backlogs?"
Safe Answer: Be Honest. "No." OR "Yes, I have one, but I am working hard to clear it."

üò∞ Panic-Recovery Sentences

  • "May I take a few seconds to think about that?"
  • "I am a little nervous, but I will try my best to answer."
  • "Could you please rephrase that question for me?"
  • "I don't know the technical term, but I can explain the logic."
